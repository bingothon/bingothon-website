Bingothon Charity Gaming For Doctors Without Borders

Bingothon is an online gaming marathon hosted on SpeedRunsLive that aims to benefit for Doctors Without Borders, but with a little twist - we are entirely focused on bingos, a particular branch of speedrunning of which we aim to raise awareness within the community.

We are dedicated to contributing in making the world a better place, by supporting the charity organization Doctors Without Borders. DWB spans internationally, all over the world, and constantly ensures that it provides as much medical care to those who need it, whether it be for emergency responses or creating more access to health care for the long term. Likewise, Doctors Without Borders constantly aims to help with medical research into issues such as tuberculosis, HIV and malaria, and make sure to apply that research when on the field. No matter where, they make sure that everything they do is done in an ethical manner, and speaking of ethics, they always make sure to be entirely transparent with their activities, and especially for instance with how they use financial resources obtained from donations and other grants. All of this means that all who contribute towards that organization help in making a difference for the better, and therefore are heroes in their own right, and can be found anywhere in the world.

Bingothon 2019 is not the first edition of the event - in December of 2018, the original “Bingothon” took place on the weekend of 1st to the 2nd of December on the Twitch channel of SpeedRunsLive, and raised over 600 US dollars that all went to Doctors Without Borders. Within those two days, we had 2 objectives: to raise money that goes to a good cause and for people, both runners and the audience, to have fun, to which this original event delivered beyond our expectations. And for this next event, and all future ones, this shall remain our philosophy, which we all hope will help in making the world a better place, both through charity, and pure entertainment.

What are Bingos?

A bingo functions with the use of a randomly generated card, with each field being an objective to fulfill, within the context of the game. When an objective is fulfilled, that part of the card is checked. In order to win a normal bingo, the player has to fill in either an entire row, an entire column, or an entire diagonal line within the card.
Now you know about the typical bingo format, but in large part to mix things up and enjoy new experiences, runners have created various other forms of bingo. These include, but are not limited to:

Blackout

The goal of blackout is to achieve every goal on the bingo card. Since this can often take a lot of time, however, most of these kinds of bingos will be done co-operatively.

Double/Triple

Instead of reaching one complete line, the goal of a double bingo is to achieve 2 lines first, while a triple bingo's goal is to reach 3 lines first.

Lockout

The goal of lockout it to achieve 13 squares as quickly as possible. The gimmick, however, is that once a square has been obtained, the other player cannot obtain it, meaning they are "locked out".

Draft

Runners begin by picking squares they would like to achieve, in a snake draft order. The snake draft is begun by one runner that picks one goal, then they alternate in picking 2 until all goals are picked. The goal of this bingo is to complete all picked squares before the opponent. Given the odd number of squares, the last goal is often not completed or completed by both players.
